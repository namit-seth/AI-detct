The deployment of machine learning models on microcontroller units (MCUs) — commonly termed
TinyML — has emerged as one of the most active frontiers in embedded systems research. This
paper presents a unified synthesis of three recent contributions that collectively address the key
challenges of TinyML deployment: (1) parallelization of neural network inference on heterogeneous
multi-core microcontrollers using embedded Rust (Ariel-ML), (2) speech recognition on highly
resource-constrained IoT platforms using 1D convolutional neural networks, and (3)
energy-efficient real-time object detection on low-power MCUs with quantized MobileNetV2. By
integrating the findings, methods, and benchmarks from these independent but complementary
works, this paper identifies unifying themes, cross-cutting trade-offs, and a consolidated design
framework for practitioners. Our synthesis reveals that quantization, multi-core scheduling, and
lightweight model architectures are the three foundational pillars for practical TinyML deployment.
We further identify convergent future research directions including on-device training, secure
over-the-air model updates, hybrid compression strategies, and memory-safe system software.
The benchmarks reported across all three works highlight that latency, flash footprint, RAM usage,
and energy consumption must be jointly optimized — a challenge that no single prior work fully
addresses in isolation.
